# Work Location Tracker

A browser extension for Chrome and Edge that tracks whether you're working from home or the office each day — so you have the data ready when filling out your monthly timesheet.

Every time you open your browser, a small popup asks where you're working. One click and you're done. At the end of the month, open the calendar to review your entries.

---

## Features

- Daily prompt dialog on browser startup (once per day)
- Persistent side panel with a monthly calendar view
- Click any past day to edit or correct an entry
- Works on Chrome and Edge

---

## Installation

### Step 1 — Download

Click the green **Code** button at the top of this page, then click **Download ZIP**.

Unzip the downloaded file into a **permanent folder** — somewhere you won't accidentally delete or move it (e.g. `Documents\work-location-tracker`).

> **Important:** Chrome and Edge read the extension files directly from this folder. If you move or delete the folder later, the extension will stop working.

---

### Step 2 — Open the Extensions page

**Chrome:** type `chrome://extensions` in the address bar and press Enter.

**Edge:** type `edge://extensions` in the address bar and press Enter.

---

### Step 3 — Enable Developer Mode

Toggle the **Developer mode** switch to ON (top-right corner of the Extensions page).

---

### Step 4 — Load the extension

Click **Load unpacked**, then select the unzipped `work-location-tracker` folder.

---

### Step 5 — Done

The extension icon appears in your toolbar. The next time you open the browser, the daily prompt will appear automatically.

---

## Daily use

- **Daily prompt** — Appears automatically when you open the browser. Click Home or Office. The dialog closes itself.
- **Calendar** — Click the extension icon in the toolbar to open the side panel with your full monthly calendar.
- **Edit an entry** — Click any past day in the calendar to change or clear it.
- **Missed a day?** — The calendar shows a "Log today's location" bar when today isn't logged yet.

---

## Requirements

- Chrome 114+ or Edge 114+ (any version from 2023 onwards)
- Developer mode must be enabled in the browser's extensions settings
